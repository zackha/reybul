Let's assemble a better future together

Our planet is the home we all share. Climate change affects everyone, directly or indirectly, and we need to act now. If we all work together – businesses, governments and people – there is still time to assemble a better future. A future that’s better for us and the planet. The United Nations’ climate conferences are central to achieving this, and 2021 saw COP26 take centre stage in Glasgow.

This was the most important COP so far

At COP26, hundreds of global leaders and thousands of government representatives, businesses and citizens came together in Glasgow. During these twelve days, the climate emergency was on top of the agenda. COP26, held between 31 October and 12 November, was generally seen to be more important than any previous climate conference. Many nations still hadn’t presented their plans to help keep 1.5°C within reach, although we see dramatic effects of climate change around the world. The years leading up to 2030 are crucial, and COP26 gave us an opportunity to accelerate our efforts.

This is what needed to be achieved at COP26

In response to the urgency of the climate crisis, the UN highlighted four major areas where progress needed to be achieved at COP26.

  1. Achieve global net-zero emissions by 2050 and keep global warming within 1.5°C of pre-industrial levels
  2. Protect communities and ecosystems from the effects of climate change
  3. Work with financial institutions to unlock the trillions in financing needed to achieve global net-zero
  4. Bring governments, businesses and people together to tackle the challenges of the climate crisis
 

IKEA supported COP26 by showing strong action

We have a responsibility to show the tangible actions we are taking here and now to tackle climate change and reduce the IKEA climate footprint. We were pleased to support COP26 in many ways across the IKEA business, engaging in the following topics:

  • How businesses can do their utmost to limit climate change to 1.5°C, by halving emissions by 2030 and achieving net-zero latest 2050
  • Enabling the many people to lower their climate footprint at home through affordable solutions
  • Supporting suppliers and the retail sector to take climate action
  • Taking climate action that has co-benefits for human health
  • The role of biodiversity and responsible forest management in both limiting and adapting to climate change

Our engagement at COP26

In the run up to COP26, and at the event, the IKEA business was present with spokespeople, key announcements and collaborations aimed at limiting climate change to 1.5°C.

  • Engaging other businesses in what true climate leadership looks like, as part of developing a global framework for being climate positive, led by WWF. In addition to reducing emissions in line with the most ambitious science-based targets, this includes other dimensions of climate leadership – such as engaging responsibly and actively in climate policy and enabling and inspiring customers.
  • Announcing a new initiative to help companies measure their impact on air pollution, together with Climate and Clean Air Coalition and Stockholm Environment Institute
  • Sharing that IKEA and other large companies have come together around a first-of-its-kind ambition statement to only purchase zero-emission fuels for ocean shipping by 2040
  • Sharing our support for the emerging nature and biodiversity agenda, and the development of science-based targets for nature via Science Based Targets Network. This will support businesses in further developing actions to reverse biodiversity loss by 2030.
  • To be able to drastically reduce our greenhouse gas emissions we must be able to forecast what actions will have the most impact. At COP26, we shared that we have built a scenario analysis tool to help us plan ahead and take the most material actions, and working to share this with others.
  • Launching SOLHETTA, our next generation of affordable LED bulbs to enable customers to lower their climate footprint

The largest IKEA retailer, Ingka Group also engaged in a range of activities, including:

  • Championing a new alliance aimed at encouraging and supporting retailers around the world to halve their greenhouse gas emissions by 2030 and achieve net-zero by 2050
  • Partnering with the Earthshot Prize, to come up with innovative solutions to the planet’s most pressing challenges, which we can then help spotlight and scale
  • Hosting an event called ONE HOME, ONE PLANET – a sustainability conference, bringing together a diverse set of thinkers, makers and influencers from around the world to discuss and co-create solutions

We are committed to becoming circular and climate positive by 2030

At IKEA, we are reassembling our business and value chain to drastically reduce our greenhouse gas emissions. To guide us on our journey, we have set ambitious goals for ourselves. They include our commitment to becoming circular and climate positive by 2030, and to reach net-zero emissions at the latest by 2050. This will make us help keep global warming below 1.5°C.

We have already made good progress, and in 2019 announced a break in the trend as the climate footprint of the IKEA value chain decreased while maintaining business growth.

The journey continues, with some key movements:

  • Our climate positive commitment includes drastically reducing greenhouse gas emissions from our value chain and removing and storing carbon through natural processes. See the total IKEA climate positive agenda.
  • We are committed to transforming into a circular business by 2030, using only renewable and recycled materials. To inspire a next generation of designers, we have recently launched a new circular product design tool.
  • Our ambition for 2030 is to inspire and enable more than 1 billion people to live a better everyday life within the boundaries of the planet.
  • We take an extended responsibility for reducing emissions from the IKEA value chain, and recently announced a new program with the intention of securing 100% renewable electricity at direct suppliers.

Responsible forest management as a tool to limit climate change

The IKEA business has spent the last year assessing how to best support the Forest Positive agenda and the 100M EUR investment announced for COP25, focussed on removing and storing carbon. This during a time when many companies are looking to back investments in forests.
